| Date | Event | Significance |

| Jun 22 | NVIDIA Pavilion at Automate 2026: Humanoid production shift | GR00T platform becoming the Android of humanoids? |

**Analysis:**
Unitree's IPO is a watershed moment. As China's leading low-cost humanoid manufacturer (with its G1 robot at ~$16K), Unitree is testing whether public markets value volume and cost leadership, or if they demand proven commercial deployments.

The **NVIDIA timing** is unmistakable. By unveiling a humanoid robot on Unitree's IPO day, NVIDIA is signaling that its GR00T platform will power the next generation of humanoids — and Unitree is a key partner. This mirrors the Android strategy: NVIDIA provides the "operating system" (GR00T + compute), while hardware manufacturers (Unitree, others) build the bodies.

**Key question the market is asking:** Can Unitree maintain its cost advantage while scaling production? The IPO valuation will tell us whether investors believe in the "China cost leader" thesis or the "US premium quality" thesis.
