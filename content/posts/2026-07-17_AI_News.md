## 🔥 Top Stories — Last 48 Hours (July 15–17)

### 1. 🇨🇳 Kimi K3: First "Open 3T-Class" Model (July 16)
**Source:** [Moonshot AI](https://moonshot.ai) · **Via:** [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/16/kimi-k3/)

Chinese AI lab **Moonshot AI** announced **Kimi K3**, their most capable model to date with **2.8 trillion parameters** — marketed as the first "open 3T-class" model. It dethrones DeepSeek's 1.6T V4 Pro as the largest openly-available model.

- **Architecture:** 2.8T total parameters (MoA — Mixture of Attention, likely with sparse activation)
- **Availability:** Web interface and API now; open-weight release promised ("open" per Moonshot's definition)
- **Benchmarks:** Self-reported results beat DeepSeek-V4 Pro and GPT-5.6 Terra across reasoning, coding, and multilingual tasks
- **Context:** Moonshot claims up to 1M token context window
- **Significance:** Represents the next escalation in the Chinese open-weight model war — DeepSeek had held the size crown with V4 (1.6T). Kimi K3 nearly doubles that. The "open" designation is disputed (Moonshot's license has restrictions), but weights are expected.

**HN discussion:** Heavy debate about whether "2.8T" uses the same sparsity math as DeepSeek (MoE with 37B active), and whether benchmarks are reproducible.

### 3. 🇪🇺 Leanstral 1.5: Mistral's Formal Verification Model (Recent)
**Source:** [Mistral AI](https://mistral.ai/news/leanstral-1-5/) · **From Paris, France**

**Mistral AI** (French, European) released **Leanstral 1.5**, a specialized 6B active parameter model focused on **formal verification and proof assistants** (Lean theorem prover).

- **License:** Apache-2.0 (fully open)
- **Size:** 6B active parameters (likely MoE with small total)
- **Performance:** Pass@1 on the Lean benchmark improved from 21.9 → 28.9; Pass@8 from 31.9 → 43.2
- **Notable:** Surpasses Opus 4.6's 39.6 pass@8 at **one-seventh the cost**
- **Availability:** Weights on HuggingFace, free API endpoint in Mistral Vibe
- **Significance:** First model specialized for formal verification proofs. Makes automated theorem proving dramatically more accessible. Represents Europe's growing specialization in vertical AI rather than general-purpose foundation models.

### 5. OpenAI GPT-5.6 Family: Luna, Terra, Sol (July 9, GA)
**Source:** [OpenAI](https://openai.com) · **Via:** [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/9/gpt-5-6/)

OpenAI's latest flagship hit general availability July 9 in three tiers:

| Model | Input Price (/1M tok) | Output Price (/1M tok) | Positioning |
|-------|----------------------|-----------------------|-------------|
| **Luna** | $1.00 | $6.00 | Budget, fastest |
| **Terra** | $2.50 | $15.00 | Balanced |
| **Sol** | $5.00 | $30.00 | Maximum capability |

**Performance:**
- Sol sets 53.6% on Agents' Last Exam (long-running professional workflows)
- Benchmarks overall: Sol ≈ Claude Fable 5 on most tasks, cheaper ($30 vs $50/1M out)
- Reasoning: Sol matches or exceeds Gemini 2.5 Pro on math/code, trails on multimodal

**Pricing strategy:** Undercuts Claude Opus ($5/$25) at the mid tier and Claude Fable 5 ($10/$50) at the top tier. Luna at $1/$6 is the cheapest frontier-class model. The three-tier naming (Sol → Sun, Terra → Earth, Luna → Moon) suggests they'll coexist rather than replace each other.

### 6. 🇪🇺 EU Forces Google to Share Search Data, Open AI on Android
**Source:** [Ars Technica](https://arstechnica.com/ai/)

The EU has ordered Google to:
1. **Share search ranking data** with competitors (expanding on DMA requirements)
2. **Open up AI capabilities on Android** — third-party AI assistants can integrate at the OS level, not just via web/app

**Significance:** This could fundamentally reshape the Android AI ecosystem. Currently, Gemini has deep OS integration; this ruling forces Google to provide equivalent APIs to competitors (Mistral, Meta, etc.). Combined with the earlier DMA ruling on app stores, Android is becoming a regulated platform.

### 7. Meta Lawsuit: AI Made Layoff Decisions
**Source:** [Ars Technica](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2026/07/lawsuit-claims-metas-layoff-decisions-were-made-by-ai-not-humans/)

A new lawsuit claims Meta's 2024–2025 layoffs were **determined by AI systems**, not human managers. The suit alleges:
- Meta used an internal "performance scoring" AI that flagged employees for termination
- Human HR simply ratified AI decisions without independent review
- Violates California law requiring human oversight for adverse employment actions

**Significance:** First major test of AI-in-HR liability. Could set precedent for how much AI can influence employment decisions. Similar to the EU's AI Act requirements for human oversight in high-risk applications.

### 9. 🇪🇺 EU Tells Meta: Disable Autoplay or Face Fines
**Source:** [Ars Technica](https://arstechnica.com/ai/)

The EU has told Meta to disable autoplay and infinite scroll on its platforms or risk "massive fines" under the Digital Services Act. The ruling targets algorithmic design features that maximize engagement at the expense of user welfare.

**Significance:** Direct regulation of AI/algorithmic design patterns, not just content moderation. Sets precedent for other platforms.

## 📊 Key Metrics & Trends

1. **Parameter race reignited:** Kimi K3's 2.8T parameters nearly doubles DeepSeek's 1.6T. But active parameters (for MoE models) matter more — Kimi's active count is unknown.
2. **European AI finding niches:** Mistral's Leanstral (formal verification) and Robostral (navigation) show European AI leaning into vertical specialization rather than general-purpose models.
3. **Regulatory acceleration:** EU is moving faster than any other jurisdiction — Android AI integration, autoplay bans, search data sharing. The EU AI Act's risk-based framework is now being operationalized.
4. **Open-weight licensing confusion:** "Open" means different things to different companies. Moonshot calls Kimi K3 "open" but with restrictions. Leanstral 1.5 is truly Apache-2.0. The market is demanding clarity.
5. **Pricing war:** GPT-5.6 Luna at $1/$6 per 1M tokens undercuts everyone. Claude Opus at $5/$25. The race to the bottom continues.
